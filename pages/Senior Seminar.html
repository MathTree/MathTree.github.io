Senior Seminar

This class is a class you take to apply what you've learned. The way it was structured for me was that there are two assignments. One problem that you have to solve, and one article of your choice (if approved) that you get to present. I chose:

▸ Problem - Let n be a positive integer such that 2n has 28 (positive) factors and 3n has 30 factors. (I had about 4 or so days to do it)
⁃ Find the smallest such n
⁃ Determine the number of factors of n
⁃ Generalize
▸ Proof of Bertrand's Postulate - I chose to present an article I found of Erdős' proof of Betrand's Postulate
⁃ Best proof I've ever read so far.
⁃ Learned a lot about this Paul Erdős.
⁃ Had to flesh out a lot of inbetween details on pen and paper.
⁃ Applied the postulate to cool graph theory things.
