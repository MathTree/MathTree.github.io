Calculus II

Calculus II starts where Calculus I ends. Still developing a lot of important, fundamental, useful and cool ideas. This course introduces Integrals and gives a deeper meaning to Calculus I

▸ Integrals & Integration Techniques - What are integrals and how do you evaluate them?
⁃ Substitution Rule
⁃ Integrals With Trig Functions
⁃ Partial Fractions
⁃ Comparison Test
⁃ Fundamental Theorem of Calculus - Theorem that ties differentiation and integration together into one
▸ Applications of Integrals - Very useful applications of what has been learned so far.
⁃ Numerous area problems
⁃ Center of Mass
⁃ Arc Length
⁃ Axes of Revolution
▸ Series and Sequences - Genuinely still not sure why this is commonly taught here. They're important, but I feel like it's extra fun.
⁃ Sequences and Series - Basic facts and definitions
⁃ Assorted Tests For Convergence
⁃ Power Series and Taylor Series
⁃ Binomial Series
