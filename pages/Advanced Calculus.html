Advanced Calculus

I have heard that there are many interpretations of "Advanced Calculus". From my understanding, this tries to be coordinate-free calculus that can be abstracted to any dimension.

▸ Vector and Calculus III Review - Review on vectors from previous classes
⁃ Parameterization review
⁃ Line Integrals and independence of path
⁃ Applied Physics problems
▸ Jacobians and Determinants - A more deep walkthrough of Jacobians and Determinants
⁃ Jacobian definition and visual representation
⁃ Flux
⁃ Applied problems
▸ Theorems - Amazing theorems that are generalizations of the fundamental theorem of calculus.
⁃ Gauss's Theorem
⁃ Green's Theorem
⁃ Divergence Theorem
⁃ Three dimensional Stokes' Theorem
▸ Differential Forms - Mathematical objects
⁃ Differentiation of the forms
⁃ Integration of the forms
⁃ Algebra on the forms
⁃ Generalized Stokes' Theorem

Relationship

▸ Calculus III
Knowledge of Calculus I, II, and III is needed. This class takes them as prerequistes and builds upon them.
▸ Linear Algebra
Knowledge of Linear Algebra is needed. This class uses a lot of vectors, matricies, determinants and all of their properties heavily.
