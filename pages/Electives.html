Electives

This is just the conglomeration of some electives you can take at SUNY Fredonia, not all

▸ Combinatorics - Concerned with 'counting', or arrangements, configurations, or combinations of some elements and with properties of some finite structures.
⁃ The addition, multiplication and pigeon-hole principles
⁃ Permutations and combinations, partitions and distributions
⁃ The binomial and multinomial theorems
⁃ Generating functions, recurrence relations, and principle of inclusion-exclusion
⁃ Combinatorial algorithms or designs
▸ Geometry - Study of absolute, Euclidean, and hyperbolic geometry from synthetic and analytic viewpoints
⁃ Axioms for geometries
⁃ Transformations
⁃ Triangles and other basic shapes, and constructions
⁃ Some consideration given to finite, spherical, and spatial geometry
⁃ Use of geometry software
▸ Interest Theory - A rigorous treatment of the mathematical theory associated with financial transactions
⁃ Simple and compound interest
⁃ Annuities and bonds
⁃ Yield rates
⁃ Amortization schedules, and sinking funds
⁃ Capital asset pricing models
⁃ Portfolio risk analysis
▸ Number Theory - Study of integers and their properties
⁃ Divisibility
⁃ Primes
⁃ Congruencies
⁃ Multiplicitive functions
⁃ Quadratic residues and quadratic reciprocity
⁃ Diophantine Equations

Relationship

▸ Calculus II
Any of these courses will require at least Calculus II. Not neccesarily content-wise but professors seem to want you to have 'been around the block a few times'.
