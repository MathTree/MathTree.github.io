Statistics

This course provides an introduction to the collection, analysis, interpretation, and presentation of data.

▸ Basic Definitions - Useful definitions that will be used througout the course
⁃ Introduced to mean, median, and mode
⁃ Introduced to the idea and definition of standard deviation and variance
⁃ Introduced to random processes, random variables, probability and sample space
⁃ Probability rules such as complement rule and addition rule for disjoint events
▸ Analyizing Qualitative Data - Analyzing data that isn't numerical
⁃ One Proportion Inference and p-values
⁃ Binomial probabilities - Includes binomial random variables
⁃ Two sided p-values, error, and power
⁃ Exact Binomial Power Calculations
⁃ Normal model, normal random variables, and the Central Limit Theorem
⁃ One sample z-test, z-interval, confidence level
⁃ Sampling from a finite population - Dealing with bias and the practical significance and cautions in inference
▸ Analyzing Quantitive Data - Analyzing numerical data. Very similar to Qualitative methods.
⁃ The normal model
⁃ Skewed Data
⁃ Sampling distributions for x̄
⁃ One sample t distributions
⁃ Prediction intervals
⁃ Bootstrapping
